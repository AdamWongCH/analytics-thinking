# Solution Guide: Case Study

---

## Purpose

This is a reference to illustrate what a strong response may look like.

There is no single correct answer.

---

## 1. Problem Definition

A strong response clearly defines the problem:

* declining student engagement
* potential impact on performance and retention

---

## 2. Data

Relevant data may include:

* logins and activity
* assignment submission
* forum participation
* student feedback

---

## 3. Methods

Possible approaches:

* clustering → identify engagement segments
* classification → predict at-risk students
* time series → monitor engagement trends
* NLP → analyse feedback

A strong answer explains *why* each method is used.

---

## 4. Outputs

Examples:

* dashboards for instructors
* alerts for at-risk students
* summary reports

---

## 5. Insights

Examples:

* low engagement students identified
* workload concerns from feedback
* declining trend in participation

---

## 6. Recommendations

Examples:

* targeted intervention for low-engagement students
* improved clarity in assignments
* monitoring engagement weekly

---

## Key Note

Strong answers:

* connect methods to decisions
* justify choices
* focus on practical impact
