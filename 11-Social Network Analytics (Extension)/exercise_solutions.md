# Exercise Solutions: Understanding Networks

---

## 1. Most Connected Student

The most connected student is the one with the highest number of connections.

This student:

* interacts with many others
* appears central in the network

---

## 2. Isolated or Less Connected Students

Isolated or less connected students:

* have few or no connections
* may appear at the edge of the network

---

## 3. Groups or Clusters

Clusters are groups of students who are more connected to each other than to others.

These may represent:

* discussion groups
* friendship groups
* study clusters

---

## 4. Interpretation of Patterns

The network structure provides insight into how students interact.

### Highly connected students

These students may:

* be more engaged
* participate actively
* influence others

---

### Isolated students

These students may:

* be less engaged
* lack interaction opportunities
* require support or intervention

---

### Clusters

Clusters suggest:

* sub-groups within the network
* possible segmentation of behaviour
* uneven participation across students

---

## Reflection

### Why are some students more connected?

Possible reasons include:

* higher engagement
* stronger communication skills
* leadership roles
* course requirements

---

### What does isolation indicate?

Isolation may suggest:

* disengagement
* lack of participation
* possible risk of falling behind

---

### Role of highly connected students

Highly connected students may:

* act as hubs of interaction
* influence participation
* help connect others

---

### How can this support decisions?

Network insights can support:

* identifying students who may need intervention
* encouraging peer support or mentoring
* understanding participation patterns
* improving engagement strategies

---

## Key Note

Network analysis is not about finding the “right answer.”

It is about:

* understanding structure
* interpreting patterns
* connecting insights to action
