# Exercise Solutions: Problem Framing

---

## Scenario

“We need a dashboard for student engagement.”

---

## 1. What is unclear?

Possible answers:

* What does “engagement” mean?
* Who will use the dashboard?
* What decision will be made based on it?
* What outcome are we trying to improve?

---

## 2. Ask better questions

Examples:

* What action will be taken based on this dashboard?
* What defines a student as “engaged” or “at risk”?
* What timeframe are we interested in?
* Who is the decision-maker?

---

## 3. What might the real problem be?

Example:

* Identifying students with low engagement early
* Supporting interventions to improve retention

---

## Extension

“We need to track course performance.”

Possible interpretation:

* Understand which courses have low completion or satisfaction
* Identify areas for improvement in course delivery

---

## Key Reflection

* Vague requests lead to unclear outcomes
* Without a clear problem, analysis may not lead to action

---

## Note

There is no single correct answer.

A good response:

* clarifies ambiguity
* connects data to decision
* focuses on outcomes rather than outputs
