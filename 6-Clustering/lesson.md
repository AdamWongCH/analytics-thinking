# Lesson: Clustering

---

## Objective

Understand how clustering groups similar observations.

---

## The Common Mistake

Many learners expect:

* a “correct answer”

But clustering:

* has no ground truth
* requires interpretation

---

## What is Clustering?

Clustering is used to:

* group similar observations
* discover patterns
* segment data

---

## Example

We want to group students based on:

* logins
* forum activity

---

## Key Idea

Observations that are similar are grouped together.

---

## K-Means Clustering

A common method:

* choose number of clusters (k)
* assign points to clusters
* update cluster centres

---

## Important Considerations

* choice of k matters
* clusters must be interpreted
* results are not definitive

---

## How this connects

In the exercise, you will:

* think about grouping

In the lab, you will:

* apply clustering and interpret results

---

## Key Takeaway

Clustering is about insight, not correctness.
