# Model Answer: From Data to Decision (Example)

---

## Observation

Students B and D show low engagement:

* low or zero logins
* no assignment submission
* low or no forum activity

Student C shows moderate engagement, while Student A shows consistently high engagement.

---

## Interpretation

Low engagement across multiple indicators (logins, submission, forum activity) suggests that Students B and D may be at risk.

Student C shows some engagement but limited participation, which may indicate early signs of disengagement.

Student A appears engaged and is unlikely to require intervention.

---

## Decision

Students should be prioritised as follows:

* **High priority:** Student D
* **Medium priority:** Student B and Student C
* **Low priority:** Student A

---

## Action

* **Student D:** Immediate intervention (e.g. outreach, support check-in)
* **Student B:** Follow-up and monitoring
* **Student C:** Light monitoring and encouragement
* **Student A:** No action required

---

## Key Insight

Multiple signals provide a clearer picture than a single metric.

Engagement should be assessed across:

* activity
* participation
* completion

---

## Reflection

Different rules or thresholds could lead to slightly different decisions.

This highlights that:

* data does not automatically determine outcomes
* interpretation plays a key role
* decisions involve judgment under uncertainty

---

## Conclusion

The analysis shows that Students B and D are most at risk.

Based on this, targeted intervention should be prioritised for these students.

The key is not just identifying patterns, but using them to decide what to do next.
