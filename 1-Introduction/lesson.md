# Lesson: Introduction to Data Thinking

---

## What is Data?

Consider the number:

**37**

What does it mean?

It could be:
- a temperature  
- a student’s age  
- the number of applications  

By itself, **data has no meaning**.

Data is:
> Raw, unprocessed facts without context.

Only when we organise and interpret data does it become useful.

---

## What is Information?

When data is processed and given context, it becomes **information**.

For example:
- “37 students applied for this course”  
- “The temperature is 37°C”  

Now the data has meaning.

> **Information = Data + Context**

---

## What is Data Mining?

Data mining is the process of transforming data into useful information.

It involves:
- discovering patterns  
- identifying relationships  
- extracting insights  

But more importantly:

> **Data mining is about finding patterns that support decision-making.**

Not all patterns are useful — only those that help us act.

---

## Types of Analytics

Analytics can be broadly categorised into four types:

### 1. Descriptive Analytics — *What happened?*
- Summarises past data  
- Example: “Student engagement dropped last week”

---

### 2. Diagnostic Analytics — *Why did it happen?*
- Explores causes and relationships  
- Example: “Engagement dropped because assignment workload increased”

---

### 3. Predictive Analytics — *What will happen?*
- Uses historical data to predict outcomes  
- Example: “This student has a high risk of dropping out”

---

### 4. Prescriptive Analytics — *What should we do?*
- Recommends actions based on insights  
- Example: “Send intervention email to at-risk students”

---

In many courses, the focus is on descriptive and predictive analytics.

In practice, the real value comes from **prescriptive thinking**.

---

## From Data to Decision

The true value of analytics lies in moving from data to action.

We follow this flow:

**Data → Information → Insight → Decision → Impact**

---

### Step 1: Data
Raw facts  
- Logs, transactions, records  

---

### Step 2: Information
Processed and structured data  
- Reports, summaries, dashboards  

---

### Step 3: Insight
Meaning derived from information  
- Patterns, trends, relationships  

---

### Step 4: Decision
Actions taken based on insight  
- Interventions, strategies  

---

### Step 5: Impact
Outcomes of decisions  
- Improved performance, reduced risk  

---

## Common Pitfall

Many people stop at:

> **Dashboards (Information)**

But:

> **Information alone does not create value — decisions do.**

---

## Key Takeaway

> The goal is not to do analysis.  
> The goal is to **think with analysis**.

---

## Summary

- Data by itself has no meaning  
- Data mining transforms data into useful information  
- Analytics can be descriptive, diagnostic, predictive, or prescriptive  
- The ultimate goal is to move from data → decision → impact
