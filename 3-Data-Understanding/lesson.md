# Lesson: Data Understanding

---

## Objective

Learn how to explore and understand a dataset before analysis.

---

## The Common Mistake

Many people jump straight into modelling.

But without understanding the data:

* patterns are misinterpreted
* models become unreliable

---

## What is Data Understanding?

Data understanding involves:

* exploring structure
* summarising variables
* identifying patterns
* checking data quality

---

## Key Questions

When you see a dataset, ask:

* What does each column represent?
* What type of data is it?
* Are there missing values?
* Are there unusual values?

---

## Types of Data

* Numerical (e.g. logins)
* Categorical (e.g. submitted: yes/no)

---

## Descriptive Statistics

Summaries help you understand:

* average
* spread
* distribution

---

## Example

A student with:

* 0 logins
* no submission

may indicate disengagement.

---

## How this connects

In the exercise, you will:

* interpret small data examples

In the lab, you will:

* explore a dataset using tools

---

## Key Takeaway

Understanding your data is the foundation of all analytics.
