# Lab: Data Understanding

---

## Objective

Explore a dataset and understand its structure, patterns, and data quality before doing any modelling.

---

## How this lab connects

In the lesson, you learned how to understand data.

In the exercise, you interpreted small data examples.

In this lab, you will work with a dataset to explore its structure, identify patterns, and generate initial insights.

---

## Scenario

You are given a dataset of student engagement, including:

* number of logins
* assignment submission status
* forum participation

Your task is to understand the data before using it for further analysis.

---

## Tasks

### Step 1: Explore the Data

* View the dataset
* Understand what each column represents
* Check the size and structure of the data

---

### Step 2: Summarise the Data

* Generate summary statistics
* Identify patterns in the data
* Look for students with very high or very low activity

---

### Step 3: Check Data Quality

* Identify missing values
* Identify unusual values (e.g. very low or zero activity)
* Consider what these values might mean

---

### Step 4: Generate Initial Insights

* What patterns do you observe?
* Which students may be at risk?
* What additional data might be useful?

---

## Deliverable

Write a short summary covering:

* Structure of the dataset
* Key patterns
* Data quality issues
* Initial insights

---

## Tools

### Python (Recommended)

* Use `lab/python/data_understanding.ipynb`

---

### R (Optional)

---

## Reminder

Understanding data comes before modelling.

Do not model what you do not understand.
