# Lesson: Prescriptive Analytics

---

## Objective

Understand how to move from predictions to decisions.

---

## Why This Matters

A model can tell you:

* who is at risk
* what may happen

But it does not tell you:

> **what to do next**

---

## The Common Mistake

Many learners stop at:

* model accuracy
* prediction outputs

But real-world impact comes from:

> decisions and actions

---

## What is Prescriptive Analytics?

Prescriptive analytics determines:

> **the best action to take, given constraints**

It answers:

* Who should we act on?
* What is the best use of limited resources?

---

## Worked Example

You have:

* 100 students
* risk scores from a model

But:

* you can only intervene on 20 students

---

### The Decision Problem

You cannot act on everyone.

You must choose:

> **which students to prioritise**

---

### Simple Approaches

#### Naive

* choose randomly

---

#### Better

* choose highest risk

---

#### More Realistic

Consider:

* risk level
* likelihood of improvement
* available resources

---

## Key Concepts

### Constraints

Limitations on what you can do

* time
* manpower
* budget

---

### Objective

What you want to achieve

* reduce attrition
* improve engagement

---

### Trade-offs

Choosing one option means not choosing another

* prioritising one student excludes another
* focusing on high risk may ignore easier wins

---

## Interpretation

Prescriptive analytics helps answer:

* Who should we act on first?
* What decision leads to the best outcome?
* How should resources be allocated?

---

## Where This Fits

Within CRISP-DM:

* **Evaluation** → compare possible actions
* **Deployment** → implement decisions

Prescriptive analytics bridges the gap between:

> **prediction and action**

---

## When to Use This

Use prescriptive analytics when:

* resources are limited
* decisions require prioritisation
* actions have consequences

---

## Common Pitfall

A common mistake is to:

* assume higher prediction = higher priority
* ignore constraints
* ignore feasibility of action

---

## How this connects

In the exercise, you will:

* prioritise students under constraints

In the lab, you will:

* apply decision rules to select actions

---

## Key Takeaway

Predictions inform decisions.

But:

> **decisions create impact**
