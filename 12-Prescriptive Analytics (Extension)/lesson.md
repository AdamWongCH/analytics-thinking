# Lesson: Prescriptive Analytics

---

## Objective

Understand how to move from predictions to decisions.

---

## Why This Matters

A model may tell you:

* who is at risk
* what may happen

But it does not tell you:

> what to do next

---

## The Common Mistake

Many learners stop at:

* model accuracy
* prediction outputs

But real-world impact comes from:

> decisions and actions

---

## What is Prescriptive Analytics?

Prescriptive analytics helps determine:

* the best action to take
* given constraints and objectives

---

## Worked Example

You have:

* 100 students
* risk scores from a model

But:

* you can only intervene on 20 students

---

### The Problem

Who should you prioritise?

---

### Possible Approaches

#### Naive

* choose randomly

---

#### Better

* choose highest risk

---

#### Even Better

* consider:

  * risk
  * likelihood of improvement
  * available resources

---

## Key Concepts

### Constraints

Limitations on what you can do

* time
* manpower
* budget

---

### Objective

What you want to achieve

* reduce attrition
* improve engagement

---

### Trade-offs

Choosing one action means not choosing another

---

## Interpretation

Prescriptive analytics helps answer:

* Who should we act on?
* What is the best use of resources?
* What decision leads to the best outcome?

---

## When to Use This

Use prescriptive analytics when:

* resources are limited
* decisions need prioritisation
* actions have consequences

---

## Common Pitfall

A common mistake is to:

* assume higher prediction = higher priority
* ignore constraints
* ignore practical feasibility

---

## How this connects

In the exercise, you will:

* prioritise actions

In the lab, you will:

* apply decision rules to a dataset

---

## Key Takeaway

Predictions inform decisions.

But:

> **decisions create impact**
