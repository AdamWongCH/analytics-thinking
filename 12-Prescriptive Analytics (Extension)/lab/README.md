# Lab: Prescriptive Analytics

---

## Objective

Decide which students to prioritise under real-world constraints.

---

## How this lab connects

In the lesson, you learned how prescriptive analytics moves from prediction to decision.

In the exercise, you prioritised students using simple rules.

In this lab, you will apply decision rules, account for budget constraints, and compare different ways of selecting students for intervention.

---

## Scenario

You are given a list of students with:

* predicted risk scores
* estimated intervention costs

Your team has a limited budget.

Your task is to decide:

* who should be prioritised
* how to make the best use of limited resources

---

## Tasks

### Step 1: Explore the Data

* Review student risk scores and intervention costs
* Identify possible trade-offs

---

### Step 2: Apply a Simple Rule

* Rank students by risk
* Select students using a basic prioritisation rule

---

### Step 3: Add Constraints

* Introduce a budget limit
* Select students while staying within the available budget

---

### Step 4: Improve the Decision

* Compare risk-first and value-based approaches
* Evaluate which rule makes better use of resources

---

### Step 5: (Optional) Try Optimisation

* Compare your heuristic approach with a simple optimisation-based solution

---

## Deliverable

Write a short summary covering:

* decision rule used
* trade-offs considered
* effect of the budget constraint
* comparison of different approaches
* implications for real-world decision-making
