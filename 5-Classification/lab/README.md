# Lab: Classification

---

## Objective

Build and evaluate a classification model to support decision-making.

---

## How this lab connects

In the lesson, you learned what classification is.

In the exercise, you explored decision trade-offs.

In this lab, you will build a model, evaluate its performance, and interpret the results in a decision context.

---

## Scenario

You are given student engagement data.

Your task is to predict whether a student is at risk.

---

## Tasks

### Step 1: Explore the Data

* Understand the variables
* Identify input and target variables

---

### Step 2: Build the Model

* Train a classification model

---

### Step 3: Generate Predictions

* Predict at-risk vs not at-risk

---

### Step 4: Evaluate Performance

* Examine the confusion matrix
* Calculate accuracy, precision, and recall

---

### Step 5: Interpret Results

* What types of errors does the model make?
* Are these errors acceptable?

---

### Step 6: Reflect on Decisions

* Which metric matters most in this scenario?
* Is the model useful for decision-making?
* What trade-offs exist?

---

## Deliverable

Write a short summary covering:

* Model performance
* Types of errors
* Trade-offs between precision and recall
* Decision implications
