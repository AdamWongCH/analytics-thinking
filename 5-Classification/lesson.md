# Lesson: Classification

---

## Objective

Understand how classification models assign observations to categories.

---

## The Common Mistake

Many learners focus on:

* model accuracy

But miss:

* what errors matter

---

## What is Classification?

Classification is used to:

* predict categories
* assign labels

---

## Example

We want to predict:

Is a student:

* At risk (1)
* Not at risk (0)

---

## Key Idea

A classification model learns patterns that separate groups.

---

## Predictions vs Reality

Predictions are not always correct.

We need to evaluate:

* how often the model is correct
* what types of mistakes it makes

---

## Confusion Matrix

A confusion matrix shows:

* True Positive
* False Positive
* True Negative
* False Negative

---

## Why This Matters

Not all errors are equal.

Example:

* Missing an at-risk student → serious
* Flagging a safe student → less serious

---

## How this connects

In the exercise, you will:

* think about classification decisions

In the lab, you will:

* build and evaluate a classification model

---

## Key Takeaway

A good classification model is not just accurate.

It makes the *right kinds of mistakes*.
