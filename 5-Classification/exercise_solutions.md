# Exercise Solutions: Classification

---

## Outcomes

“At risk” may refer to:

* low engagement
* low performance
* likelihood of dropout

---

## Errors

Missing an at-risk student is usually worse because:

* no intervention is triggered

False positives are less severe but may waste resources.

---

## Context

If resources are limited:

* precision becomes more important

If missing risk is critical:

* recall becomes more important

---

## Note

Evaluation depends on context, not just metrics.
